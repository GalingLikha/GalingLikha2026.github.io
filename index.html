About the Recognition Program

What is Galing Likha?

Galing Likha 2026 AWDA is a standards-based recognition program — not a cultural competition. It does not pit one culture against another. Instead, it recognizes weaving communities and partner LGUs that demonstrate distinction in: authenticity and cultural safeguarding, craft excellence, fair livelihood outcomes, sustainability, market readiness, and enabling local governance support.

Jointly issued by the Department of the Interior and Local Government (DILG) and the Philippine Commission on Women (PCW), Galing Likha 2026 AWDA is a countrywide initiative that transforms weaving from a heritage craft to a strategic development pathway — empowering local governments to invest meaningfully in women-led livelihoods through the 5% LGU GAD budget, potentially unlocking PhP 80 billion in support for weaving ecosystems.

"The result is a nationally coherent system where culture, enterprise, and governance reinforce one another."

Selection Process

Island Secretariats for Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao receive and validate all entries. They confirm the 12″×12″ woven swatch is received and correctly labeled, verify all three signed documents (Community Consent, Authenticity Declaration, Benefit-Sharing Statement), and forward qualified entries to the Island-Wide Selection Committee (IWSC). Incomplete or non-compliant entries are not forwarded.

The Island-Wide Selection Committee (IWSC) evaluates all qualified entries using the six evaluation criteria. The IWSC selects the Top 5 finalists per island group. Finalists make presentations before the IWSC. After presentations, the IWSC selects the Top 2 Island-Level Excellence Awardees per island group who advance to the National Finals. Bottom 3 finalists receive ₱30,000 each.

The National Selection Committee (NSC) evaluates the 6 national finalists (Top 2 per island group). National finalists must present the weave AND at least one physical Heritage-to-Use design application (clothing, accessories, interior items, etc.), demonstrating responsible translation of the living heritage into contemporary use. The NSC selects 3 National Distinction Awardees — one per island group — who receive ₱150,000 each and the Amazing Weaves Distinction Award, with showcase at the ASEAN Trade Show, Grand Ballroom, Okada Manila.

Eligibility Rules

✅
Any LGU may join as Lead Proponent with a Community Partner (association / cooperative / cluster / recognized community)
✅
One entry per LGU featuring one flagship weave tradition for the recognition cycle
✅
The featured weave must be produced within the LGU's jurisdiction — indigenous weaves prioritized
✅
Swatch must be exactly 12″ × 12″ (1 square foot) and labeled with entry code — couriered to Island Secretariat
❌
Entries using imported finished textiles or non-local production are ineligible
❌
Entries without Community Consent and all signed mandatory documents will not be evaluated
❌
Misrepresentation, unauthorized motif use, forged endorsements, or plagiarism → disqualification at any stage

Ethical Safeguards

Community Consent & Endorsement

The weaving community must give full, informed consent to participate, including awareness of benefit-sharing terms.

Authenticity & Attribution Declaration

All cultural narratives, motifs, and attribution claims must be accurate and not misrepresent the intellectual heritage of other communities.

Benefit-Sharing Statement

A mutually agreed arrangement must be in place ensuring weavers directly and equitably benefit from prizes, recognition, and commercialization.

All three documents must be signed by both the Local Chief Executive and the authorized Community Partner representative.

Sacred & Restricted Motifs — Special Protocol

For entries featuring restricted or sacred motifs and traditional knowledge: entries must declare whether commercial use is permitted and provide authorization from recognized custodians/elders as applicable.

Restricted motifs may be showcased for recognition subject to community protocol; however, no entry shall present restricted motifs as commercially available without explicit custodial consent.

This provision protects the cultural sovereignty of indigenous communities and is a mandatory declaration in the AWDA application form.
